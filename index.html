Airfoil Aerodynamics: CFD Analysis of the NACA 2410 Using ANSYS Fluent

Conducted a computational fluid dynamics (CFD) study of the NACA 2410 airfoil to investigate aerodynamic behavior across laminar and turbulent regimes. Built refined meshes and varied angles of attack between -45° and 45° to analyze lift, drag, and stall onset. Simulations were validated against experimental data, demonstrating accurate prediction of aerodynamic efficiency and flow separation patterns. The project reinforced key fluid mechanics principles and enhanced proficiency in advanced CFD methodologies.

Electromagnetic Motor: Applying Circuit Theory to Mechanical Motion

Designed and analyzed a DC motor that converts electrical energy into mechanical motion using fundamental circuit principles. Applied Ohm’s Law, Kirchhoff’s Laws, and DC circuit analysis to determine current flow, resistance, and efficiency in motor operation. Explored the relationship between voltage, current, and magnetic field strength to optimize performance, reinforcing both theoretical understanding and practical application of electromagnetism in engineering design.

Wing Structure Analysis: Optimizing Aerodynamic Efficiency

Conducted a comprehensive structural and aerodynamic analysis of a representative aircraft wing using computational fluid dynamics (CFD). The project involved modeling airflow patterns, calculating lift and drag forces, and evaluating structural responses under different load conditions. This work emphasized the integration of simulation tools with engineering principles to optimize performance, improve design reliability, and validate theoretical predictions.
